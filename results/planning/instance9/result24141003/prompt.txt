For instance, if the object is plastic, it cannot be placed in a box containing compressible objects (foldable or bendable), ensuring the integrity of the plastic object.

The `bend` method allows the robot to bend 1D bendable objects, provided they are not plastic, while the `fold` method applies to 2D foldable objects, again excluding plastic ones. Both actions require the robot's hand to remain empty before and after execution, emphasizing that these actions do not involve holding the object.

The initial states of the objects are as follows: `object0` is a plastic 3D cuboid, `object1` is a rigid 3D cylinder, `object2` is a foldable 2D circle, and `object3` is a bendable 1D line. None of these objects are initially in the box. The goal is to have all objects packed into the box, as indicated by the "Packed" state being `True` for each object.

Overall, the system is designed to simulate a robotic arm's interaction with various objects, considering their physical properties and constraints, to achieve a specific goal state where all objects are packed into a box.
